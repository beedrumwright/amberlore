AMBERLORE

by Baird Lupine

Hark…

❦ ❦ ❦

From far in the leas

To burghs that gleam,

From a mind gone too quiet

To a bairn to dream.

Deep in the trees

Far from the palace walls

There's a small, silent voice

Telling us to risk it all.

Near a decaying grove masked by flames of the forgotten, behind the dark silence of the tumultuous world before us lay a child sent by the stars themselves to tell a story, a chronicle of time and of the ones who kept it. Wielding nothing but a fresh page of parchment and a bottle of ink, Saturn set to writing.

The stories he fabricated were as different as they were the same; tales of feckless, irksome characters, of munificent minds that captured the world around them, and of sagacious mortals who could recite more than the gods themselves. Among them were gods, mortals, and monsters alike, all equipped with different goals and ever-changing faces.

But, the most certain of all things was that these spirits, these tremendous, turbulent characters gave their stories to share with that decaying grove..in hopes that it just might blossom.

With each name he wrote came another wonder just waiting to be explored. Tales of vengeance, of heartbreak, family, and the overwhelming sense of longing for nothing but to belong. Their minds floated up with the determination of ravens and the grace of doves, subconsciously — or perhaps unconsciously altogether — sharing a story that was destined to be told. One way or another.

Welcome

❦ ❦ ❦

AMBERLORE is a multimedia song-cycle and legendarium being built by Baird Lupine — songs, prose chapters, character bibles, a hand-drawn map, and a kingdom called Lupinous. This is a quick tour.

What is it?

A song-cycle told by nineteen narrators called raconteurs, each with their own song and prose chapter, plus twelve interludes for the people in between. The whole thing is set in a world called Altalune, made by a creator-god named Saturn who used to be a bullied teenager from a planet that no longer exists. There is a prophecy. There is a kingdom. There is a girl who does not know what she is.

Who's behind it?

Baird Lupine — a thirteen-year-old writer working on her first long project. AMBERLORE has been in development for years, in notebooks and on guitar and in hand-drawn maps. This site is the place it lives now while it grows.

What's already here?

Six completed songs on guitar and vocals. Character bibles for all nineteen raconteurs and several interludes. A hand-drawn map. The opening of Silvia's chapter and Christopher's chapter. Side vignettes that live outside the spine of the legendarium. More arrives every month.

About

AMBERLORE is a multimedia project created by Baird Lupine. It includes songs, writings, artwork, and performances drawn from various inspirations but all focusing on one clear thesis:

In times of struggle, there is triumph. In times of triumph, there is struggle. There is wonder in grief and findings in loss.

And there is life all around us.

Each raconteur has a different core idea. Some focus on love after grief, or grief after love, or how horrible junior high is. But they are all deserving of a read and your attention.

Every voice will always matter.

That's why they were written.
