# AMBERLORE

AMBERLORE is a multimedia project consisting of literary writings, songs, and art created by Baird Lupine. It tells the stories of twenty-two main characters along with interludes from others, showing the world completely through the character’s eyes and giving them the chance to be the raconteur. 

Most all stories are set in a renaissance or medieval timescape; in various places throughout the universe of Altalune. Throughout the stories, more questions will be raised while others will be answered, yet the essence is to bring to light what so many miss: the importance of recognition. And with that, AMBERLORE will bring its readers the desire to crack open that dust covered book that lies in the corner of their shelves, with a worn and heavy title; “Present”

AMBERLORE is organized chronologically, by the births and significant events of each raconteur.
There is no end, nor a beginning to these stories. It will continue long after the final word is read; AMBERLORE is much more than a story, after all.

Along with a chance to retell their own tale, the raconteurs will be equipped with their own songs (which can be found in their title page) and a brief introduction from Saturn.

The important thing to realize about AMBERLORE, though, is that every story is worth reading. Through twenty-two different sets of eyes, the morality of black and white will begin to fade away like dust in the wind.
AMBERLORE reminds mortals with the minds of children that the discovery of wonder will never truly vanish, but only collect dust until they’re ready to use it again.

The song the bell chimes will become more faded as they habituate, but there will come a day when the tune will ring a special sort of rhyme, and they will remember the marvel they once wielded.
