AMBERLORE

by Baird Lupine

Hark…

❦ ❦ ❦

From far in the leas

To burghs that gleam,

From a mind gone too quiet

To a bairn to dream.

Deep in the trees

Far from the palace walls

There's a small, silent voice

Telling us to risk it all.

Near a decaying grove masked by flames of the forgotten, behind the dark silence of the tumultuous world before us lay a child sent by the stars themselves to tell a story, a chronicle of time and of the ones who kept it. Wielding nothing but a fresh page of parchment and a bottle of ink, Saturn set to writing.

The stories he fabricated were as different as they were the same; tales of feckless, irksome characters, of munificent minds that captured the world around them, and of sagacious mortals who could recite more than the gods themselves. Among them were gods, mortals, and monsters alike, all equipped with different goals and ever-changing faces.

But, the most certain of all things was that these spirits, these tremendous, turbulent characters gave their stories to share with that decaying grove..in hopes that it just might blossom.

With each name he wrote came another wonder just waiting to be explored. Tales of vengeance, of heartbreak, family, and the overwhelming sense of longing for nothing but to belong. Their minds floated up with the determination of ravens and the grace of doves, subconsciously — or perhaps unconsciously altogether — sharing a story that was destined to be told. One way or another.

Welcome

❦ ❦ ❦

AMBERLORE is a multimedia song-cycle and legendarium being built by Baird Lupine — songs, prose chapters, character bibles, a hand-drawn map, and a kingdom called Lupinous. This is a quick tour.

What is it?

A song-cycle told by nineteen narrators called raconteurs, each with their own song and prose chapter, plus twelve interludes for the people in between. The whole thing is set in a world called Altalune, made by a creator-god named Saturn who used to be a bullied teenager from a planet that no longer exists. There is a prophecy. There is a kingdom. There is a girl who does not know what she is.

Who's behind it?

Baird Lupine — a thirteen-year-old writer working on her first long project. AMBERLORE has been in development for years, in notebooks and on guitar and in hand-drawn maps. This site is the place it lives now while it grows.

What's already here?

Six completed songs on guitar and vocals. Character bibles for all nineteen raconteurs and several interludes. A hand-drawn map. The opening of Silvia's chapter and Christopher's chapter. Side vignettes that live outside the spine of the legendarium. More arrives every month.

Timeline

The Long Unfolding

❦ ❦ ❦

The cosmology of Altalune, told in order. From before the world to the war that comes after the song-cycle.

Before time

The In-Between

Older than Altalune. Alstroemeria the Birchweaver tends it. Souls who die come here to rest before being reborn. None are rushed. None refuse the return.

Cerulean's end

The boy who became a god

Lyra Zenith Rinne was a bullied teenage outcast on the planet Cerulean. He wrote stories for solace. Cerulean exploded. He woke in the In-Between.

Alstroemeria pitied him and transformed him into Vattalea Saturn — the Creator. Using the pre-written stories of his mortal life as a blueprint, Saturn wove the world of Altalune.

The First Age

The slate, wiped

Saturn watched what his stories had become. Disappointed, he wiped the slate. The First Age ended. The Sixrines — an ancient order who worked with Old Magic — existed in this age, and most of them are gone now.

The Three

The Desrosiers siblings

Three traveling musicians became the first new Vattalea after Saturn's anger had cleared. Jackson on lute and vocals drowned and became Vita, Vattalea of the Sun and Life. Leah on violin was poisoned and became Reika, Vattalea of Rain and Weather. Annabelle on pan flute died in a rockslide and became Archemoros, Vattalea of Nightmares and the Moon.

The Second Age

The Valoyi arrive

Mortals whose powers bond to a specific trait or emotion — Ashlin's art-magic activates when she thinks of something happy; Vyne's growth-magic sprouts when they get nervous; Silvia's chains rise when love and rage twist into one.

The Sixrines come back into the light. Katarina Liadon, one of the last remaining, finds a Grimoire that contains only the prophecy. She becomes Fenris of Jaxon Hill, the Terror, and begins the hunt.

The Trials

The wrong child trained

Fenris was the political architect of the Valoyi Trials. Believing the prophecy's child must be a Valoyi, she manipulated the kingdom of Lupinous into participating in the mass-burning of suspected Valoyi children. Many died. Sarah Nilrilith was one of them.

Sarah's spirit became Defessus, the small witness, who now inhabits the Firefly Tree. Silvia was taken in by Fenris and trained as if she were the prophesied child. The training was extractive; Silvia became a destructive monster instead of the soul-offering child the verse promised. The wrong child was trained.

The right child — Roxanne — remained unseen, hidden in an orphanage no one in the kingdom would think to look at.

Twelve years before now

Christopher counts stars

Christopher Holloway, seventeen, in Timberton with his older brother Bethwood. The events of his chapter "Catching Falling Stars" take place here.

Five years before now

The Golden Swan

Silvia, twelve, and Sarah, eight, arrive at the Golden Swan in Mysthaven after fleeing Drearhollow. Kaiso Lunar takes them in. Silvia meets Ashlin — five years before Ashlin will ever meet Silver.

A year before now

The castle, the bond, the loss

Silvia is sent undercover to assassinate Queen Natalia Everglade of Mysthaven. The mission fails, but Christopher hides her during her months in the castle — which is how their bond forms.

Sometime during her castle years, Silvia accidentally kills Kaiso. She does not yet know that her older sister Silver killed their parents.

Now

AMBERLORE

The song-cycle. Leon Lekha Arthur kills Christopher in a storage closet of the castle. Silvia binds Leon to the clock on the front of Mysthaven Castle in purple chains — protection that came too late, made manifest — for the kingdom to see. She walks into the burning castle.

Henry Evrin duels Silver and shoots her left eye out. Indigo names herself Silver three times on the floor of a rented house; Ashlin arrives and names her Silver three times within thirty seconds.

In Symphon (Act Two)

The lives braid together

After the origin chapters end, the raconteurs' lives intersect. Nick and Silvia meet at a bar in lower Mysthaven and slowly build a life. To Breathe in a Billow of Smoke is their novella. Silvia and Nick eventually almost-adopt Calliope, Vyne, and Lilac Evans — chosen family with no paperwork, arrived at through small daily choices. Christopher is dead by then, but Silvia carries him in her hallucinations, and later meets his Vattalea form: Nerondil.

Other raconteurs braid in their own ways. The ensemble movement holds each intersection as its own piece.

Attingere (Act Three)

The gathering, the war

The nineteen raconteurs are planned to meet — Heroes of Olympus-style — to face an antagonist still being shaped. Roxanne is expected to discover what she is in that book, not the earlier ones.

The song-cycle assembles the souls. The novellas braid them together. The sequel makes them fight.

Glossary

A Small Dictionary

❦ ❦ ❦

The cosmology of Altalune has its own words for things. Here is a small dictionary of the ones that matter.

The Realms

There are three.
AltaluneThe world Saturn created. The kingdom of Lupinous is its named heart. Where most of AMBERLORE takes place.
The In-BetweenOlder than Altalune. Tended by Alstroemeria the Birchweaver. Souls who die come here to rest before being reborn — neither rewarding nor punishing, simply pausing. Even the Vattalea will eventually return.
OritariaMordai Temerity's underworld. Souls only end up here via bargain — Mordai cannot take a soul, only trade for one. The Specters are those who have been here and returned, marked.

Peoples & Magic

The kinds of beings in the cosmology.
VattaleaThe gods of the cosmology — mortals who died and were given work, holding cosmological domains, guiding others. Saturn, Vita, Reika, Archemoros, Defessus. Not a reward, a job.
ValoyiMortals whose powers bond to a specific emotion or trait. The power can only be summoned when the feeling is reached. Ashlin (joy), Vyne (anxiety), Silvia (love-and-rage).
SixrinesAn ancient order that existed in the First Age and worked with Old Magic. Most are gone. Fenris of Jaxon Hill — once Katarina Liadon — is one of the last.
SpectersThose who bargained with The Firebringer and were transformed by the cost. They have been to Oritaria and returned, marked.
NightwalkersBeings of the nightmare realm. Archemoros is the Prime Nightwalker.

Faiths

Religions in Lupinous read the cosmology differently.
VattaliansThe mainstream faith of Lupinous. Believe straightforwardly in rebirth via the In-Between.
TwilistsThe faith of the Holloway family. Forbid romantic relationships before age twenty. Rooted in a belief that young souls need time to settle into their new bodies before bonding.
FountistsPeople who have pledged their lives to the spirits of the caves and taken a vow of musicality. Each chooses one specific instrument at the moment of their vow, and the bond is irrevocable — for the eternal soul, not just this body. Homeland is Caliant.

Concepts & Things

Words for the things that matter.
The ProphecyFound by Katarina Liadon (Fenris) in a Grimoire that contained only this one verse. The engine of AMBERLORE. Names a child between stardom and solace — Roxanne, though no one knows.
The Valoyi TrialsFenris's mass-burning of suspected Valoyi children, framed as cultural sanctification, run with the consent of the political class. Sarah Nilrilith died here.

The shape of a song-cycle

AMBERLORE is structured as a song-cycle in the lineage of Yaelokre by Cole Seymour — a built world with characters who each get their own song, framed by a narrator-creator. The overture Hark! opens the cycle, with each raconteur receiving a two-line couplet in Saturn's voice. Then each raconteur gets a full song and a prose chapter. The twelve interludes hold the breathing between.

Six songs are completed on guitar and vocals: Hark!, Torrential (Reika), Plena Luna (Archemoros), Clipped (Naomi), The Shape of Silence (Silvia), and Confession (Charlotte). Thirteen more raconteur songs are to come.

The Memento Protocol

The legendarium document — the master file that holds the cosmology, the prophecy, the map, the overture, the raconteur bibles, and the prose chapters as they're drafted — is named for a practice: writing things down so that they can be returned to. Like the Appendices to Tolkien or The Silmarillion alongside its parent work, the legendarium is the externalized memory of the project.

This document will be updated as AMBERLORE grows.

The hand-drawn map

The map of Lupinous on the Map page is a digital interpretation. The canonical version is hand-drawn — pencil on paper, every place named in Baird's hand. The digital version was made to share more easily, but the original chart remains the source.

Character bibles & inspiration sheets

Each of the nineteen raconteurs has a "A Bit About..." sheet and a separate Inspiration sheet. The Bit-About sheet holds the standardized fields — full name, species, age, song, residence, family, role, instrument. The Inspiration sheet pulls from a wide library of characters and works that share an aesthetic with the figure: Yaelokre, Ride the Cyclone, The Owl House, Encanto, Tangled, Stranger Things, Nimona, EPIC, Demon Slayer, Bluey, and many more.

Several raconteurs carry quiet personal connection to Baird's family: Saturn carries their oldest brother, Silver Gray their father, Caleb Jackson their second brother, Vyne their third brother (and structurally Baird's own gender dysphoria), Lilac their younger sister. And Charlotte (Charlie) Windfellow was Baird's first-ever original character — the originating particle of AMBERLORE, and the reason the legendarium exists at all.

Aesthetic lineage

AMBERLORE draws from a cluster of works that share certain qualities: song-driven, queer-coded or queer-affirming, magic-is-real-and-painful, family-is-the-wound-and-the-cure. The four primary aesthetic cosmoses that recur across the inspiration sheets are Yaelokre (the song-cycle-with-built-world form), Ride the Cyclone (the dead-teenagers-telling-their-stories register), The Owl House (magical-misfit-teen), and Encanto (family-as-wound-and-cure).

The three acts

AMBERLORE unfolds in three acts.

Act One: AMBERLORE is the raconteur origin cycle — nineteen Hark! chapters, each raconteur telling their own past in their own voice. The amber in the title is a preservation metaphor: each chapter is the fossilized formative event that shaped the person who will appear in Act Two.

Act Two: IN SYMPHON is the ensemble movement — pieces where raconteur lives braid together. The "what happens next" stories. Nickvia's novella To Breathe in a Billow of Smoke lives here. So do the intersection pieces where raconteurs meet, form chosen families, and continue their arcs beyond their origin chapters.

Act Three: ATTINGERE is the collective climax — the reaching-toward. When all nineteen voices converge on a single conflict. The antagonist is still being shaped. Roxanne is expected to discover what she is in that book, not the earlier ones.

Tolkien did this (Hobbit → Lord of the Rings). Riordan did this (Percy Jackson → Heroes of Olympus). Pullman did this (Northern Lights → His Dark Materials). The first act is not the war. It is the making of the people who will fight it.

About

The Author

❦ ❦ ❦

Baird Lupine is a writer and songwriter. AMBERLORE is their first long work — a multimedia song-cycle and legendarium with nineteen raconteur voices, twelve interludes, six completed songs, and a kingdom still being mapped.

The project draws from a wide library — Yaelokre, Mistborn, His Dark Materials, Song of Achilles, Studio Ghibli, Heroes of Olympus, EPIC, Gorillaz — but is its own world entirely. New material arrives monthly. Songs are released as they are recorded.
